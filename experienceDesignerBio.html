In my childhood, whenever my kid sister or I were having trouble with our school work, our grandmother would always tell us, "Make it into a game! Find the pieces that interest you. Start playing with them. Before you know it, you're having fun finding out how they all fit together. But more importantly, you're learning!"

As a self titled Experience Designer and Developer, I've chosen to see and do things with VR from a different perspective. Games, movies, concerts, a majestic view from atop a mountain peak, and even those stagnant moments of having to be at work are all different ways of experiencing our individual realities. Doesn't matter if those realities are of the virtual or the real-real, each come with varying forms of interaction.

This choice of seeing and doing is a personal belief system. As we know by looking all across nature, and as grandma still points out, games are so much more than a way of having fun. They can provide a positive means of installing an endless list of things we may need throughout our lives, and a way of honing the installed traits and skills as well as preexisting ones.
